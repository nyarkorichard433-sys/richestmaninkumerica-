The Mission

This is not a book
about money.
It is a book about who you were
always meant to become.

The New African Dream is a groundbreaking series that takes the African reader on a single, continuous journey — from the first financial law applied to a single salary, to a business built and transferred across generations, to the civilisational transformation that becomes possible when an entire generation decides to build.


Written by a pharmacy technician from Kwadaso, Kumasi — not a banker, not an economist, not a celebrity — this series proves that the laws of wealth work for everyone who applies them, in every city, at every income level.


Set in Kumerica — where Bantama is Philadelphia, Adum is Manhattan, and Kwadaso is Florida — this is the African financial literacy framework the continent has been waiting for.

The Author

Richard
Nyarko

CEO, Rich-Dad Foundation • Financial Literacy Advocate • Cardano Enthusiast

Richard Nyarko was born in Kumasi, Ghana — in Kwadaso, Florida — the neighbourhood that became the heart of the Kumerica framework. A pharmacy technician and graduate of KSTU, he spent five years earning a salary and saving nothing. Then he found the laws.

He did not write this series as a wealthy man looking back. He wrote it as an ordinary person looking forward — applying the same laws he is teaching, in the same city where his readers live, with the same income his readers earn.

Richard is the founder and CEO of the Rich-Dad Foundation, a financial literacy organisation dedicated to empowering young Africans with the knowledge and tools to build lasting wealth across generations.
